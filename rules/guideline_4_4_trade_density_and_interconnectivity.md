# Trade Density and Interconnectivity (Topic 4.4)

## 1. Classic Traveller (Book 3 & 6) Logic
* **Route Generation**: Primarily driven by **Referee Fiat** rather than automated math. 
* **Communications Routes**: Generally established at Jump-2 or Jump-3 distances. X-Boat routes follow these paths and are capable of Jump-4.
* **Philosophical Constraint**: Routes should be drawn to avoid making the entire subsector accessible; "Backwater" areas must be preserved for exploration.
* **Trade Classifications**: Attributes like Agricultural (Ag) or Industrial (In) are used as narrative indicators of commercial interest but do not trigger procedural traffic lots in the core text.

## 2. Mongoose 2nd Edition (WBH & Core) Logic
* **Economic Complementarity**: Routes are established by matching worlds with complementary Trade Codes (e.g., an Industrial world matching with an Agricultural world).
* **The 4-Parsec Rule**: Pairs must be within 4 parsecs of each other with an available Jump-1 or Jump-2 path to form a trade route.
* **The "Main" Bonus**: Being located on a **Jump-1 Main** or established trade route grants a $DM +1$ to the Starport Class and potential positive DMs to Population generation.
* **World Trade Number (WTN)**: A calculated metric based on Tech Level, Population, and Starport used to determine the volume of passenger and freight traffic.
* **Data Reference**: **Trade Routes Table** (matching codes) and **Passenger/Freight Traffic Tables**.



## 3. Traveller 5 (T5) Logic
* **Importance (Ix) as Anchor**: Interconnectivity is a direct result of a world's Importance. 
* **Established Routes (J-4)**: Worlds with high Importance are linked by routes of Jump-4 or less. 
* **Intermediate Connections**: If a direct J-4 link between two Important worlds is impossible, the route "hops" through intermediate, less important worlds.
* **Traffic Scaling**: The Ix digit is the primary variable for determining the daily and weekly volume of starship arrivals.
* **Data Reference**: **Expected Ship Traffic Table** (maps Ix to ship volume).

## 4. RTT Engine Logic
* **Desirability-Led Growth**: Does not use trade routes or "density" scores to establish links.
* **Expansion Probability**: The likelihood of a world being inhabited (and thus connected) is a function of its **Desirability Score** and the **Settlement Time** (in centuries) of the overarching culture.
* **Colony/Outpost Logic**: 
    * **Colonies**: Formed if $2D6 - 2 \le \text{Desirability}$.
    * **Outposts**: Formed if $1D6 - 1 \le (TL - 9)$.
* **Constraint**: Commerce is an emergent result of habitation, not a procedural driver of it.
